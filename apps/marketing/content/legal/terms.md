## 3. Definitions

The following defined terms are used throughout these Terms:

- **"Appearance"** means a single court appearance, hearing, conference, or call scheduled in a New York State court proceeding for which a Litigator posts coverage via the Platform.
- **"Appearance Fee"** means the per-appearance fee set by the Litigator when posting an Appearance, paid to the Per Diem for their services.
- **"Technology Platform Fee"** means the fee charged by Benchline to the Litigator for use of the Platform, currently set at fifteen percent (15%) of the Appearance Fee (e.g., if the Appearance Fee is $200, the Technology Platform Fee is $30, for a total charge to the Litigator of $230). This is a fee for technology services provided by Benchline and is explicitly **not** a share of, or division of, legal fees within the meaning of New York Rules of Professional Conduct ("**NY RPC**") Rule 5.4.
- **"Total Charge"** means the Appearance Fee plus the Technology Platform Fee.
- **"Posting"** means a listing created by a Litigator on the Platform describing an Appearance available for coverage.
- **"Claim"** means a Per Diem's acceptance of a Posting.
- **"Outcome Report"** means the structured report submitted by the Per Diem following an Appearance, describing what occurred at the proceeding.
- **"Stripe"** means Stripe, Inc., Benchline's third-party payment processing provider.
- **"Stripe Connect"** means Stripe's multi-party payment infrastructure used to facilitate payments between Litigators and Per Diems.
- **"Content"** means any text, data, documents, files, photographs, or other materials submitted to the Platform by a User.

> **[NOTICE: For attorney review]** The Technology Platform Fee definition and the disclaimer distinguishing it from a fee-sharing arrangement under RPC 5.4 is critical. Please confirm this framing is sufficient under the NY RPC and that no further structural safeguards are required.

## 6. Postings and Claims

### 6.1 Litigator Postings

When creating a Posting, the Litigator must provide accurate and complete information, including: court name, index number (may be omitted for conflict-check purposes if the case is under seal), date and time of Appearance, matter type, relevant instructions, opposing counsel identification (for conflict-of-interest screening), and the Appearance Fee. Litigators are solely responsible for the accuracy of Posting information.

### 6.2 Conflict-of-Interest Screening

Benchline performs automated conflict screening by comparing the opposing counsel identified in a Posting against the profiles of Per Diems. A Per Diem associated with opposing counsel will be blocked from viewing or claiming that Posting. **This automated check is not a substitute for each attorney's independent professional obligation to conduct a conflicts analysis.** Both Litigators and Per Diems remain solely responsible for complying with their independent professional obligations under NY RPC Rule 1.7 and related rules.

> **[NOTICE: For attorney review]** Review the conflict-check mechanism and confirm the disclaimer is sufficient, or whether more robust manual-check procedures should be required before a Claim is finalized.

### 6.3 Claims

By claiming a Posting, a Per Diem represents and warrants that: (a) they hold an active New York bar admission in good standing; (b) they carry required malpractice insurance; (c) they have independently confirmed they have no conflict of interest with any party to the matter; and (d) they are able to appear at the scheduled date, time, and location.

### 6.4 Cancellations

Cancellation policies are set out in the [Litigator Agreement](#). In general: (a) Litigators may cancel at no charge until a Posting is claimed; (b) after a Claim, a 50% fee applies to cancellations within 24 hours of the Appearance; and (c) a 100% fee applies to no-shows. Per Diems who cancel within 24 hours of an Appearance without good cause may be subject to account suspension.

## 7. Payment Terms

### 7.1 Payment Processing

All payments are processed through Stripe Connect. Litigators must add a valid payment method before posting an Appearance. By authorizing a charge, you agree to Stripe's [Terms of Service](https://stripe.com/legal) and [Connected Account Agreement](https://stripe.com/connect-account/legal). Benchline is not a bank, money transmitter, or payment processor; payment services are provided solely by Stripe.

### 7.2 Charge Authorization

When a Litigator posts an Appearance, Benchline will authorize the Total Charge (Appearance Fee plus Technology Platform Fee) to the Litigator's payment method. The charge is captured (settled) once the Per Diem submits the Outcome Report and the 24-hour review window closes without a dispute filed by the Litigator, or when the Litigator explicitly confirms completion, whichever is earlier.

### 7.3 Technology Platform Fee

The Technology Platform Fee is currently fifteen percent (15%) of the Appearance Fee, representing compensation to Benchline for the technology services provided, including platform hosting, bar verification, conflict screening, AI processing, matching technology, payment infrastructure, and related services. This fee is paid by the Litigator to Benchline. It is **not** withheld from the Per Diem's Appearance Fee. The Per Diem receives the full Appearance Fee set by the Litigator.

*Example: Litigator sets Appearance Fee at $200. Benchline charges the Litigator $230 ($200 + $30 Technology Platform Fee). The Per Diem receives $200.*

### 7.4 Payouts to Per Diems

Per Diems receive payouts via Stripe Connect to their connected Stripe account. Standard payout timing is subject to Stripe's standard transfer schedule. Per Diems may opt into **Stripe Instant Payouts**, subject to a 1.5% fee on the payout amount, which fee is passed through to the Per Diem at cost. Benchline does not mark up the Instant Payout fee.

### 7.5 Taxes
